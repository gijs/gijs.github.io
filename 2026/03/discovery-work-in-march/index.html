Discovery work in March

2 Mins

The discovery board is where ideas live before they become work. In March we had a full ideas column and actually managed to move several things through to validation — which doesn’t always happen.

The dominant theme this month was storage by level. Multiple water authorities have been asking for a way to manage storage volumes not just geographically, but as a function of water level: how much water can this area hold at 45cm? At 60cm? Right now that kind of analysis requires exporting data and doing it in Excel. We want to bring it into Rana directly.

What moved forward

The in-app feedback form reached the Quick Designs stage. We’ve been relying on email and informal channels for user feedback, which means we hear mostly from power users and miss the quieter majority. A lightweight, contextual feedback mechanism inside the product should help.

The app-logo menu exploration was a quick design sprint to figure out what should live behind the main navigation entry point. The current menu has grown organically and needs pruning. We sketched three different information architectures and did a light validation round with two internal users.

What stayed in Ideas

A few things are still sitting in the backlog, not quite ripe. The combined map view — showing outputs from two different processes side by side — is technically doable but the interaction model is thorny. We’ve seen it done poorly in other tools and don’t want to rush it.

Notifications also remain unsettled. The question isn’t really about technical implementation; it’s about what’s worth notifying. A publication being shared is obvious. A simulation completing at 2am is less clearly a notification-level event for most users.

Sprint outcome

The team completed the sprint with no issues unresolved — something the Zenhub sprint review duly noted with a small congratulatory message. These things matter more than they probably should.
