Conceptual Integrity
In 1975, Fred Brooks said: I will contend that Conceptual Integrity is the most important consideration in system design. It is better to have a system omit certain anomalous features and improvements, but to reflect one set of design ideas, than to have one that contains many good but independent and uncoordinated ideas. In 1995, Brooks still hasn’t changed his mind: I am more convinced than ever. Conceptual Integrity is central to product quality.
